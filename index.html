Why & Why Now?

From our own experiences, those of our networks, and the wider working community reflected through myriad articles and posts, hybrid and remote work is not working out. The potential benefits to employers and employees are not being realised.

As an engineer, in a team, we're in a new world - post pandemic. But our tools, and to an extent, our processes and ways of working haven't really changed. There are just more: more tools, more meetings, more metrics, and as a result/side effect, more noise and more distractions.

As a manager, I'm limited in what I can see. I can't really just watch how my team interacts anymore. Are folks talking, helping each other?

As a CTO / business leader, how do I measure how my teams are actually performing. And how are they interacting? Are we creating silo's, and are we actually incentivising our values?

The Raft Solution

A(n online) platform for remote and hybrid teams to interact, collaborate, with (enhanced) visibility and cohesion(, where the way teams interact and work together is made visible).

  • Rafts ("virtual rooms")

    A virtual room / space for each team to interact. This would include seeing what team members are working on, a place to pair program, or ask for help. A place to have design discussions, and a way to celebrate other team members. Think a second monitor, bring a number of the existing collaboration tools together (not rebuilding). Here we track some key interactions so that we can reward collaboration.

  • Manager / team leader context

    A manager context (also visible to the team member) on the positives a team member is bringing to the team. How much time is focussed vs not. Are they mentoring other, more junior members? How many tasks are they assisting on? Do their actions correspond to their career goals, and the company values?

  • Enterprise context

    An enterprise (for want of a better term) context, where we can, using the collected signals, provide SPACE like metrics - but more importantly (we think): the social graph of the organisation. Not what we want it to be, but what it is in practise. We can provide visibility on key communicators and influencers, especially those that fly under the radar - and hopefully, use this to design better, happier cultures and organisations.
